Top 5 Medications in the USA

Lisinopril

Lisinopril is the generic drug name for Zestril. It is a blood pressure medication in the angiotensin converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitor group of drugs.

Doctors prescribe lisinopril to treat high blood pressure in adults and children 6 years old and older. People may also take lisinopril with other medications if they have heart failure. Doctors may also prescribe the drug for someone who has experienced a heart attack.

Levothyroxine

People with hypothyroidism take levothyroxine (Synthroid). This medication is an artificial form of thyroxine (T4), a hormone that comes from the thyroid gland. T4 is low in people with hypothyroidism. Doctors may prescribe levothyroxine to adults and children, including newborns.

The Food and Drug Administration (FDA) also approves using levothyroxine for pituitary thyrotropin suppression in people with certain forms of thyroid cancer. Pituitary thyrotropin suppression helps prevent the growth of cancer cells.

Atorvastatin

Statin therapies, such as atorvastatin (Lipitor), are the first-line treatment option for high cholesterol.

They work by modifying how much cholesterol the body makes in the liver. Atorvastatin blocks enzymes in the liver responsible for making cholesterol.

Metformin

Simvastatin (Zocor) is another statin drug. It works in the same way as atorvastatin, and doctors prescribe it for the same reasons.
